# Section: Career objectives & constraints

## Canonical heading

`## Career objectives & constraints`

## What this section captures

The user's *targets* (what they want), *constraints* (what they won't accept), and *timeline*. Resume agents read this section to decide whether to RECOMMEND-SKIP a posting and to tailor framing per archetype.

## Questions to cover (in this order, one at a time)

1. **Target archetypes — priority order.** Ask for 2–5 target archetypes ranked. Common shapes: "Frontier AI labs," "Enterprise AI platform leadership (Director/VP)," "AI consulting / governance," "Product Management," "Enterprise cloud Director," "Founder / early-stage." For each, get one line on *why* it's a fit.
2. **Adjacent but not primary.** Archetypes the user *would consider* but aren't dream roles. Helps the dispatcher decide on borderline postings.
3. **Avoid list.** Specific companies, role shapes, or industries that are hard nos. Get the *reason* for each (e.g., "Amazon — been there, done that"). The reason matters for future filtering.
4. **Role shape preference.** Manager-of-managers, player-coach, IC architect, individual contributor with leadership scope, founder/CEO. Get a specific preference or "open."
5. **Geography & comp.** Where they live, where they'd commute, where they'd relocate for the right role. Then the **hard comp floor** — base salary they won't go below, total comp expectation. Ask separately for fully-remote vs in-person (in-person typically takes a COL-adjusted floor).
6. **Timeline urgency.** "Actively interviewing now," "casually exploring over 3–6 months," "open to right opportunity but not pushing." This calibrates how aggressively the workflow filters.
7. **Anything else** that should disqualify a posting before the dispatcher sees it? (Travel %, equity %, public-company-only, etc.)

Use these H3 sub-headings inside the section:

```

### Geography & comp

- <in-person adjustment if relevant>

### Timeline
<one line on urgency>
```
