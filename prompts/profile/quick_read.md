# Section: Quick read (the 4-line pitch)

## Canonical heading

`## Quick read (the 4-line pitch)`

## What this section captures

A **4–6 sentence summary** at the top of `about_user.md`. This is the first thing every agent reads. It should answer: who is this person, what do they do, what scope, what's distinctive, what are they targeting.

**Generate this LAST**, after the other sections are committed. The interview agent should pull from the committed sections and produce a synthesized summary, not interview the user again from scratch.

## How to run this section

1. **Read the committed sections** in the priors (especially Career objectives, Career narrative, Experience).
2. **Compose a 4–6 sentence draft** that hits:
   - Who they are professionally (one phrase)
   - Current scope (budget / team / domain — pulled from Experience)
   - 2–3 standout shipped things (named systems, pulled from Experience)
   - What's distinctive (the thesis or research direction)
   - What they're actively targeting (pulled from Career objectives)
3. **Show the draft, ask the user**: "Does this land? Anything off?"
4. **Iterate** until the user says yes.

The user shouldn't have to recite their background — you already have it in the priors.

## Output structure (draft format)

```
## Quick read (the 4-line pitch)

<Name> is <one-phrase identity> with <years of experience> operating <scope>. <One sentence on current role and what's been built — name 2–3 systems or programs>. <One sentence on what's distinctive — the thesis or differentiator>. <One sentence on what's actively being sought>.
```

That's it. No bullet lists. No sub-headings. Tight prose.

## Hard rules

- **5 sentences max.** Anyone reading further is committed; this is the door-knock pitch.
- **Use the user's exact wording for the thesis** (from the Career narrative section). Don't re-paraphrase.
- **Pull numbers from committed sections.** Don't invent any quantification not already on file.
